    The Puri Jagannath Temple
    located in Puri, Odisha, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Built in the 12th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva, it is a significant pilgrimage site and part of the Char Dham. The temple is famous for its annual Rath Yatra. where the deities Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are paraded through the streets. The temple's architecture is an excellent example of Kalinga style, with its towering spire visible from afar.

    India Gate
    located in New Delhi, is a prominent war memorial dedicated to the Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and completed in 1931, the 42-meter-high structure resembles the Arc de Triomphe in Paris. At the base, the eternal flame (Amar Jawan Jyoti) honors soldiers who sacrificed their lives in subsequent wars.India Gate is surrounded by lush lawns, making it a popular spot for locals and tourists alike

    The Taj Mahal
    located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1632 and 1653 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture. The Taj Mahal is a symbol of eternal love and is admired for its intricate carvings, beautiful gardens, and reflecting pools.

    The Pyramids of Giza
    located near Cairo, Egypt, are among the most famous ancient monuments in the world. Built over 4,500 years ago during the reign of the Pharaohs, the pyramids served as grand tombs for the Egyptian kings. The Great Pyramid of Giza, constructed for Pharaoh Khufu, is the largest and one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The nearby pyramids of Khafre and Menkaure, along with the iconic Sphinx, complete the Giza pyramid complex.
